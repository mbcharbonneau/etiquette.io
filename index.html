Wearable Etiquette

An illustrated guide for new technology.

Smartwatch

  • Don't check your notifications when talking with others, standing in line, or other situations where it would be rude to look at a "dumb" watch.
  • If you think a notification is important, it may be more suitable to pause and check your phone so others know what you're doing.

Fitness Tracker

  • It's rude to check your fitness tracker's display when chatting or walking with others.
  • Your fitness tracker should match the occasion. Some designs are appropriate for formal business dress, some for sports or exercise, and some for both.

Smartphone

  • When you're waiting for people to arrive at a bar or restaurant, leave your phone out on the table. Your friends who are already there will feel included when they text.
  • Do not check your phone while talking with someone else. If you are expecting an important notification, wait until a pause in the conversation to check it.

Google Glass

  • Don't wear your Google Glass in public situations.
  • …especially at bars or movie theaters that explicitly disallow them.
  • Wear your Google Glass while biking, skiing, or other sports.
  • If you have a disability, it may be appropriate to wear your Google Glass in more situations.

Drone

  • Do not fly your drone close to other people, pets or livestock.
  • Avoid flying near houses and land where someone would normally have an expectation of privacy.
  • Get permission before flying your drone at races and sporting events.
  • Check the map before flying your drone.

Virtual Reality Headset

  • Remove your VR headset to acknowledge when someone enters the room.
