Wearable Etiquette

An illustrated guide for new technology.

Smartwatch

  • Don't make others think you have somewhere to be. Avoid checking notifications while talking with a friend, waiting for a cashier, or in similar situations.
  • It may be more appropriate to look at notifications on your phone, instead of on your watch.

Fitness Tracker

  • Don't frequently check your fitness tracker's display while chatting or walking with others.
  • Your fitness tracker should match the occasion. Some designs are appropriate for formal business dress, some for sports or exercise. A few may be suitable for both.

Smartphone

  • When waiting for people to arrive at a bar or restaurant, leave your phone out on the table. Your friends who are already there will feel included when they text.
  • Wait until a pause in conversation before checking a notification on your phone. If the conversation is important, do not check it at all.

Google Glass

  • Don't wear your Google Glass in public places.
  • …especially at bars or movie theaters that explicitly disallow them.
  • Wear your Google Glass while biking, skiing, or during other sports.
  • If you have a disability, it may be appropriate to wear your Google Glass in other contexts.

Drone

  • Do not fly your drone close to people, pets or livestock.
  • Be conscious of other's privacy when flying your drone. Don't fly in areas where you might make someone uncomfortable.
  • Get permission from event organizers before flying your drone at races and sporting events.
  • Check the map before flying your drone.

Virtual Reality Headset

  • Remove your VR headset to acknowledge when someone enters the room.
